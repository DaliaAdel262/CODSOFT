Diana Frances Spencer, Princess of Wales

Princess Diana, often referred to as the "People's Princess," was a beloved member of the British royal family known for her humanitarian work and charisma. She captivated the world with her grace, compassion, and dedication to charitable causes, including efforts to raise awareness about HIV/AIDS and landmine victims. Diana's tragic death in 1997 sparked an outpouring of grief globally, leaving behind a legacy of kindness, advocacy, and social change.
